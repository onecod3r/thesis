### The caveat that shaped the following week

**Run-to-run variance is the same order as the subset deltas.** ME_132's two same-config xyz
runs differ by ~2.5 points (72.47 vs 74.95) while the subset effects being measured are 1–3
points. Any ablation conclusion from train-loop numbers alone is inside the noise — which is
why the canonical eval (fixed split, fixed metric, `eval_gru.py`) became a hard requirement
for leaderboard entry rather than a nice-to-have.

## 2. Analysis work

**Motion energy (07-15, TODO §1).** `gislr.0.dataset.motion-energy.ipynb` executed end to end
at three scopes — 50 videos / 10 categories / all 94,477 videos in 189 resumable chunks
(~25 min at ~65 videos/s, **0 failures**). The seeded samples reproduce the global
per-landmark pattern (rho **0.954** per-video, **0.996** per-category), so sample-based
landmark analysis is trustworthy going forward. Produced the ME-126 keep/discard
recommendation: hands 42 + upper-body pose 8 + lips 40 + eyes/nose 36.

Two secondary goals landed with it and paid off all week: **DuckDB** as the memory-bounded
loading layer over 94K parquet files, and the **resumable manifest pattern** (per-unit
artifact written before `done`, atomic saves, `failed` retried) that POPSIGN extraction now
runs on.

**Discriminability (07-16, TODO §3.0).** The follow-up instrument, measuring class
*information* rather than movement: ANOVA F-ratio, mutual information, and a probe classifier
on per-video landmark descriptors — the probe on the **same canonical split** as every GRU
run, so its numbers are comparable by construction. It confirmed both predicted blind spots
of motion energy and adjudicated the ME-126 vs FP-118 pose disagreement in ME's favor.
A new caveat of its own: marginal F cannot rank *face* landmarks (rigid-head redundancy) —
only the subset-level probe prices them.

Also delivered: `modules/dataset/landmark/subsets.py`, the canonical registry of every subset
in play (FULL_543, FP_118, ME_126, ME_132, HANDS_42, HANDS_POSE_50), with `ME_126.array`
verified equal to the trained run's `landmarks.npy`.

## 3. Infrastructure

**Registry v1 → the restructure (07-17 → 07-18).** Run folders documented themselves well for
a human and not at all for a query. `metadata.json` + `build_model_index.py` fixed that on
07-17, with an explicit `eval_status` so training-loop numbers can never masquerade as
leaderboard numbers. Alongside it: the **fresh-run-folder policy** (a finished run is never
reused; only interrupted runs resume in place) and training regime **`v2-plateau-300`**
(batch 512, lr 1e-3, epoch cap 300 + early stopping, ReduceLROnPlateau — OneCycleLR dropped
because a fixed-epoch anneal would be truncated by an early stop).

**Architecture notebooks (07-17, TODO §4).** `StreamingLSTM`, `BiLSTM` (offline-only accuracy
reference, never a deployment candidate) and `CausalConv1D` generated from the GRU notebook's
parameterized identity block. Verified by CPU smoke test — and the verification worth keeping:
for the two causal models, **corrupting frames after a sample's last valid frame provably
leaves its logits unchanged**. Streaming viability is asserted, not assumed.

**Restructure (07-18, TODO §0.4).** The four training notebooks were ~95% identical, so all
shared logic moved into `src/modules/model/` (`architectures.py` as the *single* model-class
definition imported by both training and eval — state_dicts can no longer drift). Registry v2:
flat `data/models/<epoch-seconds>/` holding only `meta.json` + weights + `assets/`, with
dataset/architecture/subset as fields rather than directory levels. Plus the
`data/{raw,cache,temp,external,models}` placement policy, CWD-independent lazy paths
(importing `modules.paths` no longer downloads anything), the `modules/scripts/` CLI split,
and the docs logs/reports split.

### The cost, stated plainly

**The registry was reset to empty and the pre-reset weights are gone.** All eight v1-regime
runs — including the FULL_543 baseline and the ME-126 leader — survive only as numbers in git
history (≤ `3668dae`) and in these logs. Six canonical evals that were pending can now never
be completed. Every headline result in §1 is therefore a **historical reference**, not a
reproducible leaderboard entry, until the grid is re-run under regime v2 into the fresh
registry. That re-run was the week's largest outstanding debt.
